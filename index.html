Who We Are

The Bao
To Me Story.

Where Heritage Meets Heart. It started as a small delivery kitchen in 2021. What it's grown into feels a lot more personal: a celebration of flavours that shaped our chef's childhood, and the family recipes that came with them.

Every dish at Bao To Me carries a story. Our baozi? Born from Chef Wan Ming Li's father's recipes, brought into the present with a little love and a lot of flavour.

We pull from the full width of Pan-Asian cuisine: the quiet precision of Chinese and Japanese cooking, the big punchy soul of Southeast Asian street food.

Three friends started this: a techie who became a chef, a corporate type who ate his way through Asia, and someone who knew how to make it all work. The rest is Bao To Me.

"It's not just about the bao — it's about bringing people together."

Our interiors take their cue from the alleyways of Southeast Asia: layered, lived-in, full of character. Metal shutters, lightbox signs, vintage fans turned into lamps. Real stuff, real charm.

Welcome to Bao To Me. Welcome Home.

Our Space

Where the
Alleyway
Comes Alive.

Think Southeast Asian alleyway, brought inside. Every corner is layered, lived-in, full of things to look at.

Metal shutters. Lightbox signs. Vintage fans that became lamps. It's raw, a little nostalgic, and very much its own thing.

A mahjong mirror, hand-painted murals, posters that made us smile: the details add up to a space that feels like somewhere you've been before, even on your first visit.
